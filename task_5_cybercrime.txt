**Topic: UPI & Online Payment Fraud**

**What it is:** UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves tricking a user into authorizing a transaction that sends money to a scammerĺs account. Unlike traditional hacking, this relies on "social engineering"Śmanipulating the victim into taking action.

**How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):**

1.  **The Hook:** A scammer contacts the victim through a marketplace (like OLX) or a fake customer service call, expressing interest in buying an item or "fixing" an account issue.
2.  **The "Receive" Trap:** The scammer sends a "Request Money" link or a QR code to the victim via a messaging app, claiming it is for the victim to *receive* payment.
3.  **The Deception:** The scammer tells the victim they need to enter their UPI PIN to "verify" or "authorize" the receipt of funds.
4.  **The Theft:** As soon as the victim enters their PIN, the money is instantly debited from their account and moved to the scammer's wallet or untraceable account.

**Target:** College students, small-scale online sellers, and individuals unfamiliar with the technical rule that **you never need a PIN to receive money.**

**Consequences:** Immediate financial loss, potential compromise of banking credentials, and psychological stress.

Part B: 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist

*Save this as `prevention-checklist.md` or `.txt` in your `task-5-cybercrime/` folder.*

1.  **PIN Rule:** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. PINs are only for sending money.
2.  **Verify Identities:** Always cross-check the identity of anyone asking for a financial transaction over a call or message.
3.  **Official Apps Only:** Download banking and UPI apps only from the official Play Store or App Store.
4.  **Enable 2FA:** Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for all social media and email accounts.
5.  **Secure Connections:** Avoid conducting financial transactions over public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks.
6.  **Report Spam:** Use the "Block & Report" feature on WhatsApp or SMS for suspicious payment links.
7.  **Limit Public Info:** Do not share your phone number or QR codes on public social media forums.
8.  **Know the Helpline:** If scammed, immediately report it to the authorities.
